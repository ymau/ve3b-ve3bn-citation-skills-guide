Exercise 1:
Understanding Citation Styles

Learn the three main academic citation styles and when to use them.

Why Academic Citations?

Academic writing requires proper citations to give credit to sources, avoid plagiarism, and help readers find your references. Different subjects use different citation styles.

Style Used In In-Text Format Reference List
APA Psychology, Education, Social Sciences (Smith, 2025) References
MLA Literature, Arts, Humanities (Smith 45) Works Cited
Chicago History, Business (Smith 2025) or Footnotes Bibliography

Exercise 2:
APA Style Citations

Practice building APA in-text citations and reference entries.

APA 7 In-Text Citation Format

Single Author: Recent studies show significant results (Smith, 2025).
Two Authors: The research indicates (Jones & Lee, 2025).
Direct Quote: "Citation is essential" (Smith, 2025, p. 45).

Build an APA In-Text Citation

Source: Book by Dr. Emily Chen, published 2025, page 78 (direct quote: "Academic writing requires precision.")

"Academic writing requires precision"

Build an APA Reference List Entry

Book: Author: Emily Chen | Title: Academic Writing Skills | Year: 2025 | Publisher: Oxford Press

Oxford Press.

Exercise 3:
MLA Style Citations

Practice building MLA in-text citations and works cited entries.

MLA 9 In-Text Citation Format

Single Author: The author argues this point (Smith 45).
Two Authors: Research shows (Jones and Lee 78).
Author Mentioned in Text: According to Smith, "citations matter" (45).

Build an MLA In-Text Citation

Source: Article by John Davis, page 123

The study demonstrates significant findings

Build an MLA Works Cited Entry
